# Modern Town

This is a game inspired by both The Sims 3 and Rimworld amoung others. It is a 2D tile-based top down view game. The player controls a family in a living town with around 200 total active agents divided into family
units. They live out the daily modern life of their family, navigating the big and the small events that make up a normal life.

# Agents

Each agent is part of a family. A family is a unit of people who are played together, and who share a common
dwelling plot in the game. They are often but not always related by blood. Each family must have at least one adult. Families can contain between one and ten agents. Families with more than six agents are uncommon.

Most agents are human but they also include pet dogs and cats.

Other animals (lizards, hamsters) are NOT agents and are treated as in game interacterable objects in a similar way to how we handle a television or radio.

# Jobs

Agents can have jobs to earn an income. They can apply for entry level roles in a range of careers. And based on choices and events that transpire they can get promoted, demoted, or fired. They can change jobs
if they wish.

Agents can also get non-career local jobs (receptionist, store clerk) and part time jobs including some that
are open to older children and teens (paper round, hotdog stand).

In addition to normal jobs agents can also start businesses. These come in a range of forms from questionable MLMs they can sign up for online, hawking essencial oils and makeup, to owning a local store.

Agents can also invest if they have enough money and gain a return.

An important distinction here is that jobs are not a quick fire way to finacial stability as they are in the Sims. Rather, agents will have to balance their budget more and may experience times when they have less income or more instability (global events can also impact this - crash in the market so chances of being laid off etc).

# School and education

Agents can go to school. Children start school at age 5 and then to go high school at age 12. They gradudate at age 18. Adults that have graduated can attend college at any time, and spend four years getting a specialist degree. Agents with degrees can also pursude a phd. There are also professional courses that agents can sign up for at night school, or as online self-study courses that may give them an edge in their field or teach them new skills.

# Languages

Agents all speak a common in game language (yet to be named) by default. They can also learn new languages as a skill. And agents that come from foreign lands to visit (via events) may speak the foreign language only, and be able to communicate with those who have learned their native tongue. Being bilingual may also open up other oppertunities.

# Life statges

Agents go:

- Baby
- Toddler
- Young Child
- Older Child
- Teen
- Adult
- Middle Aged Adult
- Elderly
- Venerable

Agents have a chance to get sick and die based on their life stage. Once they reach adult there is a small % chance of serious sickness each year. Mitigated by lifestyle choices and events. The chance is very small until they reach Elderly when the chance increases each year. It then increases rapidly in the Venerable stage.

Most agents will die in the latter Elderly stage, with around 10% making it some way into venerable.

# Life Events

Many manage life events have special events assocaited with them. These include but are not limited to:

- Birthdays
- Christmas
- New Year
- Spring Festival
- High Summer Festival
- Fall Spooky Festival
- Weddings
- Funderals
- Coming of Age for some religous groups (in game version of a confirmation or bahmitzva)
